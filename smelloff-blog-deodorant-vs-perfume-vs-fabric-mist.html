Deo vs Perfume vs Fabric Mist

50 different sprays in the chemist shop. They all promise to make you smell good. But they do completely different things.

Deodorant: underarm bacteria

Designed to neutralise bacteria under arms. Antibacterial agents + fragrance. Skin-contact only. Spraying on fabric does nothing useful.

Antiperspirant: blocks sweat

Contains aluminum compounds that temporarily block sweat glands. Apply at night for best results. Different from deo.

Body spray: light fragrance

Diluted perfume for casual use. 2-4 hour lifespan. Skin-only. Spraying on sweaty clothes makes it worse — creates a chemical + body odor cocktail.

Perfume/EDT/EDP: concentrated

For skin pulse points. Higher concentration = longer wear. Expensive (₹2000+). Masks scent but doesn't fix the underlying problem.

Fabric mist: kills cloth odor

Designed to neutralise odor molecules trapped in fabric. Zinc Ricinoleate binds to and deactivates smell molecules. Doesn't mask — eliminates.

Which do you need?

Three things: antiperspirant daily, good perfume for occasions, fabric mist for everything in between. The fabric mist is the most underrated.
